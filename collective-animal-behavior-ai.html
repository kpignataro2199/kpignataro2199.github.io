Beyond Individual Animals: How AI and Next-Generation Sensors Are Reshaping Collective Behaviour Research

March 1, 2026

In recent years, AI’s role in animal studies has expanded from identifying species and individuals to addressing more complex questions: how groups of animals behave as coordinated units. A growing body of research shows that machine learning and advanced sensing tools are now being applied not just to monitor individual animals, but to analyze collective behaviour — the ways in which animals move, communicate, and coordinate as a group.

What Is Collective Behaviour?

Collective behaviour refers to how groups of animals interact and coordinate their actions at the group level. Examples include flocking in birds, schooling in fish, and herding in ungulates. AI enables researchers to track every individual in a group over time while deriving metrics that capture group-level dynamics.

AI + Sensors: The Backbone

High-resolution cameras and lidar can capture rich data across space and time. For example:

  • Vision transformers can reconstruct individual positions even when animals occlude one another.
  • Multimodal architectures integrate visual and audio data to better resolve behaviour in complex environments.
My Perspective: A New Frontier

AI’s ability to process and interpret group dynamics at scale means researchers can now observe the forest and the trees simultaneously. This shift allows us to detect patterns and early warning signs that emerge only at the group level, leading to more humane livestock management and richer ecological insights.
