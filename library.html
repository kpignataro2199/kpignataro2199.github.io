AI Tools & Platforms

  • Wildbook (Wild Me): Wildbook Platform Overview — An open-source AI system for identifying individual animals like whale sharks and giraffes using unique patterns[cite: 10, 11, 82].
  • PoseR: PoseR Tool via Open Biology — A graph neural network tool developed by the University of St. Andrews to automate behavioral descriptions from video[cite: 83, 85].
  • PrimNet: PrimNet Primate Recognition — A facial recognition system for tracking individual chimpanzees with over 90% accuracy[cite: 15, 78].
  • MammAlps: MammAlps Wildlife Dataset — A multi-view, multimodal video dataset for training AI to recognize nuanced wildlife behaviors in the Swiss Alps[cite: 110, 118].

Research Articles & News Sources

  • Animal Emotions: AI Decodes Animal Emotions (University of Copenhagen) — Machine learning models distinguishing positive and negative emotional states in ungulates[cite: 97, 108].
  • Species Identification: Oregon State University Camera Trap Study — Research on training models with narrower objectives to boost species ID accuracy to 90%[cite: 120, 128].
  • Animal Health Market: Global AI in Animal Health Forecast — Market analysis projecting growth to $2.88 billion by 2029[cite: 131, 138].
  • Computer Vision for Ecology: Weinstein (2023) Review — A look at how individual-level analysis is essential for understanding cognition and social dynamics[cite: 29, 81].
  • Dairy Cow Monitoring: Hansen et al. (2020) — Research on automatic recognition of dairy cows using facial features[cite: 39, 79].
  • Microsoft AI for Earth: Snow Leopard Identification — Using neural networks to distinguish subtle visual differences in snow leopards[cite: 13, 80].
