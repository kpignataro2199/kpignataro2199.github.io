Using AI to Monitor Wildlife Behavior

January 25, 2026

Applied AI is revolutionizing how researchers study animals in their natural habitats. By using machine learning algorithms to analyze thousands of hours of video from camera traps, scientists can now identify specific species and individual behaviors without having to manually watch every second of footage.

These tools are especially vital for conservation efforts. For example, AI can detect patterns in animal migration or alert rangers to unusual activity in protected areas. This allows for a more proactive approach to wildlife management and helps ensure the welfare of species in the wild.

My take: The ability for AI to process massive amounts of visual data is a game-changer for wildlife biology. It frees up researchers to focus on conservation strategies rather than just data entry, ultimately leading to better outcomes for animal welfare.
