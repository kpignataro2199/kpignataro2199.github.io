The $3 Billion Animal Health Market

January 25, 2026

A recent global market report predicts that artificial intelligence in animal health is expected to reach nearly $3 billion ($2.88B) by the end of the decade. This growth is driven by rising pet ownership, demand for advanced diagnostic tools, and smart monitoring devices. The report highlights AI-powered wearables, precision livestock farming systems, and telemedicine/diagnostic solutions as key growth opportunities that support both welfare and productivity.

This trend clearly reflects how applied AI is moving beyond just research labs and is becoming more prevalent in commercial and clinical care settings, with devices ranging from wearables that track vital signs to tools that predict disease before visible symptoms may even appear.

  • • Why this matters: As investment in this market grows, we can expect more tools that utilize AI programs to improve everyday animal health management, ranging from pets to livestock. And as programs and devices improve, professionals can ensure better welfare outcomes and new data for researchers interested in behavior and wellbeing.
My Take: A Practical Reality

While it may be easy to think of AI as something abstract or futuristic, this forecast shows how AI has already become a practical part of animal care systems. And while some of the technology right now may still be in its beginning stages, through continued research and improvement, there will likely be many different emerging technologies in the coming years which may better help researchers in the field of animal behavior and welfare.
